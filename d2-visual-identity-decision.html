Decision D2

How will people recognise K1 at a glance?

Every strong product has a visual signature that works without a logo. TE has the orange. Nothing has the transparency. Apple had the white earbuds. This decision locks K1's visual signature — the consistent look across all photography, video, social media, and renders.

What this actually means in practice

When someone scrolls past a K1 image on Instagram, what makes them think "that's SpectraSynq" before they read the caption? That's what we're choosing. This affects: how you photograph the K1, how Blender renders are composed, what your social media grid looks like, and what your website hero image shows.

THE DIFFUSED GLOW
The Glow
"Always show that unique soft light the K1 makes"
The K1's Light Guide Plate produces a diffused, floating glow that no other LED product can replicate. No visible pixels, no strips — just smooth, volumetric light. Make this the hero of every image.
Used by: No one in the LED space. This light quality is physically unique to the LGP architecture.
The Dark Frame
"Hide the hardware, show only the light"
Photograph the K1 so the body disappears into darkness. Only the light output is visible — a floating plane of colour emerging from nothing. The product deliberately hides itself.
Similar to: Apple's iPod silhouette campaign. The product becomes invisible; the output becomes the identity.
Centre Origin
"Every pattern radiates from the middle outward"
All K1 effects already start from the centre (LEDs 79/80) and move outward. Make this radial motion the signature. In video, it's recognisable even on muted autoplay. In stills, it's concentric glow rings.
Similar to: Nothing's Glyph blink pattern — a consistent motion behaviour that's recognisable in peripheral vision.
Light Spill
"Always show the light reaching into the room"
Never photograph K1 in isolation. Always show its glow painting the desk, wall, vinyl sleeve, whatever's nearby. The K1 is a tool in a creative workspace, not a gadget on a pedestal. The light changes the space around it.
Similar to: TE always photographs gear in messy creative setups. The environment IS part of the identity.
15-25° BELOW EYE LEVEL / 10-15° OFF-AXIS
The Study Frame
"Always photograph from the same angle — like observing, not selling"
A consistent low-angle, slightly off-axis camera position for all imagery. The viewer is positioned as someone studying the light, not being pitched a product. This is a photography technique that works WITH any of the options above, not instead of them.
This is a technique, not a primary choice. It standardises the camera angle across all content regardless of which visual signature you pick.

Research recommendation

Primary — for stills The Glow + Light Spill
Secondary — for video Centre Origin
Technique — applied to all The Study Frame

The Glow is what's physically unique — no other product makes this light. Light Spill puts it in context so it doesn't look like "any glowing rectangle." Centre Origin gives video content a motion signature. The Study Frame standardises the camera angle across everything. Combined: recognisable light, in a real environment, from a consistent perspective, with radial motion in video.
