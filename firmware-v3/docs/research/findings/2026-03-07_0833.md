## 🔴 HIGH-VALUE FINDING

### PrismAudio: Decomposed Multi-Dimensional Rewards for Audio-Visual Generation
- **Paper:** PrismAudio: Decomposed Chain-of-Thoughts and Multi-dimensional Rewards for Video-to-Audio Generation (arXiv:2511.18833, Nov 2025)
- **Relevance:** Decomposes audio-visual quality into four independent reward dimensions — Semantic, Temporal, Aesthetic, Spatial — each with a dedicated reward function. This is the closest published architecture to the layered metric pipeline we are building.
- **Key transferable method:** Multi-dimensional reward decomposition for RL-based optimisation. Each CoT module (Semantic, Temporal, Aesthetic, Spatial) has a targeted reward function that can be optimised independently and then combined. The temporal reward specifically targets audio-visual synchrony. The aesthetic reward targets perceptual quality. This decomposed reward structure maps almost directly onto our L0-L4 layer model:
  - Semantic → L2 (audio-visual alignment)
  - Temporal → L1 (temporal coherence) + L2 (sync)
  - Aesthetic → L4 (aesthetic)
  - Spatial → L0 (frame quality)
- **Implementation complexity:** Medium — requires defining per-layer reward functions (which we're already doing), then wiring them into an RL loop. The CoT decomposition itself is a prompt engineering pattern.
- **Layer affected:** Optimisation (closed-loop strategy) + all metric layers

## 🔴 HIGH-VALUE FINDING

### RRD: Recursive Rubric Refinement for LLM-as-Judge
- **Paper:** Rethinking Rubric Generation for Improving LLM Judge and Reward Modeling for Open-ended Tasks (arXiv:2602.05125, Feb 2026)
- **Relevance:** Directly addresses the problem of building structured evaluation rubrics for LLM-as-judge systems. Our MLLM aesthetic judge currently uses ad-hoc rubrics. RRD provides a principled method to iteratively refine scoring criteria.
- **Key transferable method:** Recursive decompose-filter cycle: (1) decompose coarse rubrics into fine-grained criteria, (2) filter out redundant/misaligned criteria, (3) apply correlation-aware weighting to prevent over-representation. Achieves +17.7 points improvement on JudgeBench. For our system: start with coarse aesthetic rubric ("visual quality", "music match", "variety"), recursively decompose into specific measurable sub-criteria, filter for non-redundancy, weight by discriminative power.
- **Implementation complexity:** Low — this is a prompt engineering + rubric design methodology, not a model to train. Can be applied to our existing MLLM-as-judge pipeline immediately.
- **Layer affected:** L4 (aesthetic) — improves MLLM judge rubric quality

## Finding 3: AIGVEval — LLM-Based Multi-Dimensional Video Quality Decomposition
- **Paper:** Towards Holistic Visual Quality Assessment of AI-Generated Videos: A LLM-Based Multi-Dimensional Evaluation Model (arXiv:2506.04715, Jun 2025)
- **Relevance:** Decomposes video quality into three orthogonal dimensions (technical, motion, semantics) with dedicated encoders per dimension, then fuses via an LLM regression head with LoRA. Demonstrates that decomposed encoding + LLM fusion outperforms monolithic quality models.
- **Key transferable method:** Per-dimension feature encoders feeding into an LLM-based quality fusion model. For our system: encode frame quality (L0), temporal coherence (L1), and audio-visual sync (L2) as separate feature vectors, then use a small LLM or MLP to fuse into a single quality score. The multi-modal prompt engineering framework for connecting features to quality predictions is directly applicable.
- **Implementation complexity:** High — requires training or fine-tuning encoders per dimension. However, the architectural pattern (decompose → encode → fuse) is immediately usable with simpler encoders.
- **Layer affected:** Pipeline (overall quality fusion architecture)

## Finding 4: Beat Tracking as Object Detection
- **Paper:** Beat Tracking as Object Detection (arXiv:2510.14391, Oct 2025)
- **Relevance:** Reframes beat tracking from frame-level activation prediction to temporal object detection using FCOS adapted to 1D audio. Beats are treated as temporal "objects" with bounding intervals and confidence scores, processed through a Feature Pyramid Network for multi-scale temporal patterns.
- **Key transferable method:** (1) Feature Pyramid Network for multi-scale temporal pattern detection in audio — captures both beat-level and bar-level structure simultaneously. (2) Non-maximum suppression (NMS) for beat selection replaces Dynamic Bayesian Networks, giving a simpler and potentially faster post-processing pipeline. (3) Confidence scores per detected beat — our evaluation system could use beat confidence to weight the BeatAlign score (high-confidence beats matter more for sync evaluation than ambiguous ones).
- **Implementation complexity:** Medium — WaveBeat backbone + FPN is well-established in vision; the 1D adaptation is the novel part. Could replace or augment our existing beat detection pipeline.
- **Layer affected:** Pipeline (audio analysis) + L2 (weighted BeatAlign)

## Finding 5: VisJudge-Bench — MLLM as Visualisation Quality Judge
- **Paper:** VisJudge-Bench: Aesthetics and Quality Assessment of Visualizations (arXiv:2510.22373, Oct 2025)
- **Relevance:** First benchmark specifically for MLLMs as visualisation quality judges. Evaluates across data encoding accuracy, information expressiveness, and visual aesthetics. Demonstrates that fine-tuned VisJudge model (MAE 0.421, correlation 0.687 with human ratings) substantially outperforms GPT-5 (MAE 0.553, correlation 0.428) on structured visual quality assessment.
- **Key transferable method:** The finding that fine-tuned specialist judge models dramatically outperform general-purpose MLLMs for visual quality scoring. For our system: rather than using a general MLLM (e.g., Claude, GPT) as the aesthetic judge, we could fine-tune a smaller vision model specifically on LED visualisation quality ratings. The three-dimension decomposition (encoding accuracy, expressiveness, aesthetics) is also a useful rubric structure.
- **Implementation complexity:** High for fine-tuning a custom judge model. Low for adopting the three-dimension rubric structure with an existing MLLM.
- **Layer affected:** L4 (aesthetic judge methodology)

## Finding 6: AURA/AuraScore — Decomposed Factual Consistency + Logical Inference Scoring
- **Paper:** AURA: A Fine-Grained Benchmark and Decomposed Metric for Audio-Visual Reasoning (arXiv:2508.07470, Aug 2025)
- **Relevance:** AuraScore decomposes MLLM evaluation into (1) Factual Consistency (is reasoning grounded in perceptual evidence?) and (2) Core Inference (is each logical step valid?). Key insight: models achieve up to 92% accuracy but <45% on factual consistency/inference — meaning correct answers from wrong reasoning. Directly relevant to validating our MLLM judge's reasoning quality.
- **Key transferable method:** Two-axis evaluation of MLLM judge outputs: check that the judge's quality score is both factually grounded in what's visible in the LED capture AND logically derived. This catches "right answer, wrong reason" failures in our aesthetic evaluation pipeline. Also tests audio-visual synchronisation as a specific cognitive domain.
- **Implementation complexity:** Low — this is a validation/auditing technique for our existing MLLM judge output, not a new model to build.
- **Layer affected:** L4 (aesthetic judge validation/calibration)

## Finding 7: FineVQ — Fine-Grained Multi-Dimensional Video Quality with Subjective Ratings
- **Paper:** FineVQ: Fine-Grained User Generated Content Video Quality Assessment (CVPR 2025)
- **Relevance:** Introduces multi-dimensional fine-grained video quality assessment with 6,104 videos and 800K+ subjective quality ratings. Demonstrates that decomposing quality into multiple specific dimensions (rather than a single MOS score) dramatically improves alignment with human perception.
- **Key transferable method:** The data collection methodology — structured multi-dimensional subjective ratings at scale. For our system: when building the human evaluation dataset for calibrating our metric pipeline, use per-dimension ratings (frame quality, temporal smoothness, music match, overall aesthetic) rather than a single holistic score. The 800K rating scale demonstrates that crowd-sourced per-dimension ratings are feasible and produce better-calibrated quality models.
- **Implementation complexity:** Low (methodology adoption) to Medium (building our own rated dataset).
- **Layer affected:** Pipeline (calibration data methodology)
