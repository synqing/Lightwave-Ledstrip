## Finding 1: CAMEL — Confidence-Gated Selective Evaluation for Quality Judges

**Paper:** arXiv:2602.20670 — "CAMEL: Confidence-Gated Reflection for Reward Modeling" (Feb 24, 2026)
**Authors:** Zirui Zhu, Hailun Xu, Yang Luo, Yong Liu, Kanchan Sarkar, Kun Xu, Yang You

**One-sentence relevance:** Introduces a concrete mechanism for deciding when a quality judge's fast initial verdict is reliable vs. when to invoke expensive reflection — directly applicable to making our L4 MLLM-as-judge layer fast for easy evaluations and thorough for hard ones.

**Key transferable method:**
- **Two-stage gated evaluation:** First, a lightweight single-token preference decision is made (fast, cheap). Second, the log-probability margin between verdict tokens is computed — this margin "strongly correlates with prediction correctness" and serves as a confidence proxy.
- **Confidence threshold:** If the margin exceeds a threshold, the initial verdict is trusted. If below, a more expensive reflection pass is triggered that re-evaluates with chain-of-thought reasoning.
- **Key empirical result:** 82.9% average accuracy on RewardBench at 14B parameters, competitive with 70B models, because computational budget is concentrated on genuinely difficult cases.
- **No extra inference cost for confidence:** The log-probability margin comes free from the initial forward pass — no additional model calls needed to assess confidence.

**Concrete application to our system:**
- Our L4 aesthetic evaluation currently faces a cost-quality tradeoff: calling a large MLLM (GPT-4o/Claude) per frame-set is expensive and slow, but smaller models may be unreliable.
- CAMEL's pattern solves this: for each evaluation call, the judge first produces a quick verdict. If the confidence margin is high (e.g., the LED frame is obviously good or obviously bad), accept the verdict immediately. If confidence is low (ambiguous quality, edge case), trigger a more expensive evaluation — either a reflection pass, a larger model fallback, or a multi-sample consensus.
- This creates an **adaptive evaluation budget** within the optimisation loop: most evaluations complete fast (the majority of frames are clearly good or bad), while only the genuinely ambiguous cases incur full evaluation cost.
- The confidence signal itself is useful metadata: it identifies which parameter configurations produce "hard to judge" outputs, which may indicate the system is at a quality boundary worth exploring.
- Combines naturally with VideoJudge (2026-03-09_0900.md finding): bootstrap a small local judge, and use CAMEL's confidence gating to decide when to fall back to a larger API-based judge.

**Implementation complexity:** Low-Medium — requires access to token log-probabilities from the MLLM judge (available via most APIs). The gating logic is a simple threshold comparison. Main tuning: calibrating the confidence threshold for our domain-specific evaluation rubric.

**Target layer:** L4 (aesthetic evaluation) + Optimisation (evaluation cost reduction for closed-loop viability)

**Differentiation from existing findings:**
- VideoJudge (2026-03-09_0900.md) addresses making a *small* judge accurate via bootstrapping. CAMEL addresses making *any* judge efficient by knowing when its verdict is reliable.
- VisionDirector (2026-03-09_2010.md) addresses per-goal verification and rollback. CAMEL addresses per-evaluation confidence estimation.
- These are complementary: VideoJudge for training a small judge, CAMEL for gating when to trust it vs. escalate, VisionDirector for structured per-metric verification.
