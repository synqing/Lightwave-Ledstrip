06 The Enemy — Top 3 Candidates

Every strong manifesto names what it opposes. Not a competitor — a condition in the world. This choice cascades into the debut content sequence.

Safest — most provable
"Volume as Meaning"
Every "music-reactive" product collapses music to one number: amplitude. Loud = bright. That's not music reactivity. It's loudness reactivity. The entire signal — rhythm, pitch, timbre, structure — is ignored.
Content leads with
Phase Lock — two pulses resolving into sync
Manifesto verb
"study" — we study the full signal
Broadest — most intuitive
"Spectacle Without Substance"
RGB excess. Rainbow cycling. Every colour at once. The visual equivalent of shouting. LED products default to spectacle because spectacle requires no understanding of the signal.
Content leads with
Negative Space — what K1 does with silence
Manifesto verb
"refine" — we remove, not add
Most ambitious — hardest to execute
"Passive Consumption"
Technology demands nothing. Music plays in the background. Light does what it does. The user isn't invited to notice, interpret, or engage — only to consume.
Content leads with
The Grammar — "watching for the moment..."
Manifesto verb
"observe" — we invite active watching
