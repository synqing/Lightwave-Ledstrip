Solo
Each strip runs its own colour. No boundary mixing.
No blend
Independent strips
Strip A and B each display the effect's colours without modification at the boundary. Default mode — use when the effect already handles cross-strip colour intent.
Complementary
Opposite hues at boundary. Maximum contrast harmony.
Active 180°
180° hue shift
The boundary strip's hue is rotated 180° from the source. Cyan meets orange. Creates high-energy, visually dramatic transitions.
Analogous
Adjacent hues — subtle, harmonious blend. Barely noticeable at low spread.
30°
30° hue shift
Nearest neighbours on the colour wheel. Produces a warm-to-slightly-warmer or cool-to-slightly-cooler feel. Ideal for subtle, non-distracting boundary definition.
Split Complementary
Two hues flank the complement: 150° and 210°. Rich but less harsh than full complementary.
150° / 210°
150° + 210° shifts
A softer contrast than complementary. Strip B boundary blends between two flanking hues, creating a naturally balanced, three-tone harmony.
Triadic
Three equally spaced hues: 0°, 120°, 240°. Vibrant, balanced energy.
120°
120° hue shift
Red, purple-blue, blue — each strip rotated 120° around the colour wheel. The most visually equidistant, producing richly varied LED output.
Tetradic
Four hues in two complementary pairs. 90° step creates a square colour scheme.
90°
90° hue shift
Perpendicular pairs on the colour wheel. Green-to-purple creates maximum saturation contrast. Best with wider Spread values to smooth the transition.
Random
Randomised hue offset regenerated on each effect change or manual refresh.
0–359°
Dynamic offset
Each trigger picks a new random angle. Tap the refresh icon to regenerate without changing effects. Creates surprising, serendipitous colour pairings.

All 7 Modes — Design Reference

Solo —
Independent strips. No blending. Use when the effect already manages cross-strip colour intent.
Complementary 180°
Maximum contrast harmony — opposite hues collide at the boundary. Dramatic and energetic.
Analogous 30°
Adjacent neighbours — the subtlest blend. Barely perceptible at low Spread. Calming and cohesive.
Split Complementary 150° / 210°
Softer than complementary — two flanking hues create a three-way harmony without full opposition.
Triadic 120°
Equidistant triangle on the wheel. Three hues at equal visual weight — vivid and balanced.
Tetradic 90°
Square scheme — four colours at equal spacing. Rich and complex. Best at Spread 30–60.
Random 0–359°
Spontaneous. Regenerates on effect change or manual tap. Creates unexpected pairings — each show is unique.
