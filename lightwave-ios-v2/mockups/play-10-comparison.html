Design Decision Guidance

The central tension in all 9 layouts is LED visibility vs. control accessibility. For a party controller used one-handed in dim lighting, the thumb-reachable zone (bottom 55%) must contain the two highest-frequency actions: brightness and speed. Everything else can live behind a tap.

Mockup 08 (Theatre Mode) resolves this tension most elegantly. The LED preview IS the product — showing it at 60% makes the device feel powerful. All primary controls land in the thumb zone via the glassmorphism sheet. EdgeMixer and Expression params expand from the bottom on demand, maintaining a clean idle state.

Mockup 09 (Dual Column) is the power-user counterpart. The vertical sliders have the largest hit targets of all 9 layouts, and all 6 control categories are simultaneously visible. Recommended for users who frequently tweak expression params and EdgeMixer spread. The compact LED banner is the only compromise.

Mockup 06 (Quick Actions) is the best fallback if the team wants every control visible without any tap-to-expand interaction. It is the most information-dense option and works well for users who like to see the full state at a glance.

RECOMMENDATION: Build 08 (Theatre) as primary. Offer 09 (Dual Column) as "Power Mode" via long press / settings toggle.
