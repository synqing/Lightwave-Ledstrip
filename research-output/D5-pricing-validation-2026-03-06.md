## Juxtapositions

### 1. K1 vs. HyperCube 15: The Price Gap

[FACT] HyperCube 15 sells at $300–340 with sound reactivity and 95 patterns.
[FACT] K1 targets $449–549 — a $109–249 premium over HC15.
[INFERENCE] The K1's defensible differentiation for that premium is: (a) PLL beat tracking vs. simple frequency analysis, (b) 100+ effects vs. 95 patterns, (c) Light Guide Plate form factor vs. infinity mirror cube, (d) web-based control vs. app/hardware.
**Tension:** Is PLL beat tracking + LGP form factor worth $109–249 more than what HyperCube offers? The customer cannot A/B test beat-tracking quality before purchase. The LGP form factor IS visually unique and has no direct competitor — this may be the stronger justification than audio intelligence.

### 2. K1 vs. Nanoleaf: Category Confusion Risk

[FACT] A full Nanoleaf Lines 15-pack is ~$206 with music sync.
[INFERENCE] At $449–549, K1 is 2–2.7× the price of a comparable Nanoleaf installation.
[HYPOTHESIS] If customers mentally categorise K1 as "smart LED panels" (same shelf as Nanoleaf/Govee), the price is indefensible. If they categorise it as "music technology" or "experiential art," the comparison set changes entirely.
**Question:** What shelf does the K1 sit on in the customer's mind? This is the manifesto's job (D3/D4). Pricing and brand positioning are coupled — D5 cannot be resolved independently of D3.

### 3. K1 vs. ENTTEC DMX Pro: The "Pro Tool" Anchor

[FACT] ENTTEC DMX USB Pro costs $238–320 and includes NO LEDs, NO effects, NO audio analysis. It's just a protocol converter.
[INFERENCE] Professional lighting users already pay $238–320 for a bare interface. The K1 includes the entire stack (LEDs + effects + audio intelligence + control interface) for $449–549.
[HYPOTHESIS] If K1 is positioned against the professional/prosumer tool market rather than the consumer ambient market, $449–549 is actually competitive — you're getting the whole system for less than 2× the cost of just the interface.
**Question:** Does the Founders Edition buyer think of themselves as a professional/prosumer or a consumer? This determines which price anchor they use.

### 4. The Kickstarter Precedent

[FACT] Audiolux One launched on Kickstarter at $299 (2017) for a musician-oriented sound-reactive controller with 256 LEDs.
[INFERENCE] Adjusted for inflation (~25% since 2017), that's roughly $374 in 2026 dollars.
[INFERENCE] K1 offers significantly more capability (320 LEDs, 100+ effects, PLL beat tracking, web control, dual-strip LGP) than Audiolux One.
**Tension:** The inflation-adjusted Audiolux price ($374) sits BELOW the K1 floor ($449). But the K1 delivers substantially more. The question is whether the gap ($75–175) is justified by the additional capability, or whether $374-ish is the ceiling for what the market has demonstrated willingness to pay for this category.

### 5. Counter-Evidence: The $300 Ceiling

[FACT] No currently shipping audio-reactive experiential lighting product exceeds $340 (HyperCube 15 + stand).
[INFERENCE] The market has not yet validated a $400+ price point for this category.
[HYPOTHESIS] Captain may be creating a new price tier rather than entering an existing one. This is defensible IF the K1 creates a perception gap ("this is not the same thing as a HyperCube"). It is risky if the customer sees equivalence.
**Question:** Is there evidence from adjacent categories (e.g., Sonos at launch, Dyson at launch) where a product successfully created a new price tier in a category that previously capped lower?
