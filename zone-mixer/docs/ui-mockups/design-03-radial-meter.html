Design Rationale

Concept

The Radial Meter uses concentric arc gauges radiating outward from a central connection indicator. Each arc represents a brightness level, with sweep angle proportional to the 0-255 value. The layout maps naturally to the Zone Mixer's hierarchy: zones nested inside the master, the whole system's health at the centre.

Why arcs on a tiny screen

  • Peripheral readability. Arc length is easier to judge at a glance than bar height, particularly at 128px where bars become indistinguishable slivers. A 270-degree sweep gives a 100-pixel-long visual range even on a 40px-radius ring.
  • Information density. Four independent channels occupy concentric bands without overlapping. No axis labels needed — colour IS identity.
  • Orientation agnostic. The display reads correctly from any viewing angle on a desk, unlike vertical bars that assume a fixed up direction.

Visual hierarchy

  • Centre dot — connection status. Most critical info, most prominent position. Green or red , pulsing when disconnected to draw attention.
  • Inner arcs (zones 1-3) — the primary operational values. Ordered inside-out matching the zone numbering convention. Each uses its assigned colour: cyan , green , orange .
  • Outermost ring (master) — white arc that frames everything. Master is the envelope — it wraps the zones, which is exactly what it does functionally.
  • Bottom text — effect name or zone count. Secondary info that does not compete with the arcs.

Arc geometry

  • Arcs sweep 270 degrees (from 135 deg to 405 deg, i.e. bottom-left to bottom-right, passing through top). The 90-degree gap at the bottom leaves space for the text label and creates a natural "gauge" feel.
  • Track width is 8px at 128px scale (scaled to 32px at 4x). Gaps between rings are 3px. This gives clear separation without wasting the limited radial space.
  • Dim track backgrounds (rgba(colour, 0.12)) show the full sweep range so the user can judge how far each value is from maximum.

Colour system

  • Zone colours match the hardware LED assignments from the design doc: CH0 cyan, CH1 green, CH2 orange, CH3 white.
  • Arc brightness is constant (full saturation) regardless of the value — the sweep angle communicates magnitude. Dimming the arc colour would make it unreadable on the tiny screen.
  • Background track uses 12% opacity of the arc colour so the full ring is subtly visible without competing with the active sweep.

LVGL implementation notes

  • LVGL has lv_arc widget natively. Four arcs + one circle + one label fits comfortably in the render pipeline.
  • Arc anti-aliasing is built in. On the real AtomS3 ST7789 display at 128x128, the pixel density (213 PPI effective) makes the arcs look smooth.
  • The connection dot can be an lv_obj with lv_obj_set_style_radius(LV_RADIUS_CIRCLE) and a simple colour toggle. Pulsing via lv_anim_t on opacity.
  • Text label uses lv_label with LV_LABEL_LONG_SCROLL_CIRCULAR for effect names that exceed the available width.

Constraints met

  • 128x128 pixel canvas, black background
  • All four brightness channels visible simultaneously
  • Connection status immediately visible
  • Zone numbering: 1-indexed (Zone 1, Zone 2, Zone 3) — no Zone 0
  • Colour assignments match hardware: cyan, green, orange, white
