Design Rationale

Concept

Modelled after a miniature DJ mixer console. Four vertical fader tracks sit side by side exactly as they would on physical hardware — Zone 1, Zone 2, Zone 3, and Master. Each track is a filled bar whose height represents the current brightness value (0–255). The metaphor is immediately legible to anyone who has touched audio mixing equipment: higher bar = more brightness.

Visual Language

  • Zone 1 — Cyan (#00E5FF): Cool, forward-facing energy.
  • Zone 2 — Green (#00E676): Organic, ambient warmth.
  • Zone 3 — Orange (#FF9100): Warm, accent punch.
  • Master — White (#E0E0E0): Neutral authority, slightly wider track.

Layout Arithmetic (128px wide)

  • Left margin: 4px. Right margin: 4px. Usable width: 120px.
  • Zone tracks: 24px wide each (x3 = 72px).
  • Master track: 30px wide (25% wider, signals hierarchy).
  • Gaps between tracks: 6px (x3 = 18px).
  • Total: 4 + 24 + 6 + 24 + 6 + 24 + 6 + 30 + 4 = 128px. Exact fit.
  • Fader track top: y=16 (below status bar). Bottom: y=106 (above labels). Track height: 90px.
  • Bottom labels at y=112. Speed readout at y=124.

Status Bar (top 10px)

WiFi and WebSocket indicators use 7px glyphs at the top-left. Green dot = connected, red dot = disconnected. Minimal footprint — status should not compete with the faders for attention.

Fader Track Design

  • Dark grey track background (#1A1A1A) with 1px border (#2A2A2A) — looks recessed.
  • Filled portion rises from the bottom, colour-matched to the zone.
  • A brighter 2px "cap" line at the top of the fill simulates the physical fader knob.
  • Fill uses a subtle vertical gradient — brighter at the cap, slightly darker at the base — adding depth without complexity.
  • Numeric value rendered inside each track (centred, 7px font) so the user can read exact brightness without switching screens.

Why This Design Works on 0.85"

  • Glanceable: Four bars at different heights instantly communicate system state.
  • No text overload: "Z1", "Z2", "Z3", "M" are the only labels. The bars ARE the data.
  • Colour redundancy: Even without reading labels, zone identity is encoded in colour.
  • Proportional feedback: Fader height is linear with brightness (0–255 mapped to 0–90px). A 50% brightness reads as a half-height bar. No ambiguity.
  • Master differentiation: The wider master track breaks the visual rhythm just enough to signal its hierarchical role without needing extra labels.

LVGL Implementation Notes

  • Each track is an lv_bar (vertical orientation) inside an lv_obj container.
  • Track backgrounds: lv_style_set_bg_color(&style_track, lv_color_hex(0x1A1A1A)).
  • Fill indicator uses lv_style_set_bg_grad_dir(&style_ind, LV_GRAD_DIR_VER) for the gradient.
  • Cap line: a 2px-tall lv_obj positioned at the indicator top, or drawn in the bar's draw event.
  • Status icons: lv_canvas or pre-rendered 7x7 bitmaps.
  • Labels: lv_label with lv_font_montserrat_10 or a custom 7px bitmap font.
  • Refresh rate: update bars on scroll-wheel input events only (not continuous polling).
