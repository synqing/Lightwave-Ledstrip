Design 09 -- LED Strip Preview

Zone Mixer UI for AtomS3 (128x128 LCD) -- miniature LED strip representation with zone boundaries

4x Scale (512 x 512)
1:1 Actual (128 x 128)

Design Rationale

Philosophy

The LED Strip Preview design gives the operator a miniature physical twin of the actual 160-LED strip. Instead of abstract numbers or dots, you see a horizontal line of coloured pixels that mirrors the real hardware layout. The centre divider makes the centre-origin architecture immediately visible -- effects emanate from LED 79/80 outward. This is the only screen design that directly communicates spatial topology.

Why a Strip Preview at 128x128

  • 128 pixels for 160 LEDs -- 0.8px per LED, mapped via nearest-neighbour. Slight compression is imperceptible on a 0.85" panel but preserves zone proportions faithfully.
  • Centre divider line makes the centre-origin architecture immediately legible. The user can see symmetry (or deliberate asymmetry) at a glance.
  • Zone boundaries shown as subtle 1px vertical markers -- visible when sought, invisible when not. Same principle as Design 04's peripheral indicators.
  • Brightness-dimmed zones -- each zone section's pixel brightness scales with its brightness value. A zone at 40% appears physically dimmer than one at 92%. No numbers needed to read relative intensity.
  • Two-row strip mirrors the dual-strip Light Guide Plate (top row = strip A, bottom row = strip B, identical in zone mode).

Layout Map

  • Row 0--5 (6px): Status row -- WiFi dot at (4,3), WS dot at (123,3).
  • Row 8--13 (6px): Upper label -- "160 LED" left-aligned, zone count right-aligned.
  • Row 16--55 (40px): LED strip preview -- two rows of 128 coloured pixels, zone-shaded, with centre marker and zone boundary lines.
  • Row 58--59 (2px): Centre origin marker -- white 1px line at pixel column 63/64 (maps to LED 79/80).
  • Row 64--103 (40px): Zone info panel -- three zone rows with colour dot, label, and brightness percentage.
  • Row 108--127 (20px): Footer -- effect name and speed value.

Colour Choices

  • Zone 1 (inner): cyan #22d3ee -- the centre zones, closest to the LED strip centre origin (LEDs 54--105).
  • Zone 2 (middle): green #4ade80 -- mid-range zone (LEDs 27--53, 106--132).
  • Zone 3 (outer): orange #fb923c -- edge zone (LEDs 0--26, 133--159).
  • Centre marker: white #ffffff -- 1px vertical line at the strip's physical centre. Maximum contrast.
  • Zone boundaries: dimmed white #555555 -- visible but non-distracting 1px vertical lines.
  • Status dots: same as Design 04 -- green / red for WiFi, tri-state for WS.

Centre-Origin Visualisation

The K1 architecture mandates that all effects originate from LEDs 79/80 outward. This design makes that constraint visible: the white centre divider sits at the exact midpoint of the strip preview. Zone 1 (cyan) straddles the centre, Zone 2 (green) flanks it, and Zone 3 (orange) occupies the outermost edges. The operator can see that the light guide plate's centre is the origin, not an arbitrary point.

Performance Considerations

  • Strip rendering: 128 filled rectangles per row, 2 rows = 256 rect calls.~1.2ms
  • Alternatively, write to an offscreen buffer and blit once -- reduces to 1 draw call + 3 text draws.~0.5ms
  • Zone boundary lines: 4 vertical line draws.negligible
  • Text elements (6 total): zone labels, effect name, speed -- all static geometry, only redraw on change.
  • Partial update viable: only re-render strip rows on brightness change, footer on effect change.

Comparison with Other Designs

  • vs Design 04 (Minimal Glance): Design 04 uses abstract dots for zone brightness. This design shows the actual spatial layout. Trade-off: more draw calls, but communicates topology that numbers cannot.
  • vs numeric displays: Numbers tell you "Zone 1 = 92%". This tells you "Zone 1 is the bright cyan section around the centre, and it's brighter than the green section next to it." Spatial context is embedded in the visual.
  • Best for: operators who think in terms of "where on the strip" rather than "which zone number". Particularly useful during zone boundary adjustment or when running effects that interact across zone boundaries.
LED Strip Preview (upper half) Two rows of 128 coloured pixels at y=18..55. Each pixel maps to ~1.25 LEDs via nearest-neighbour. Zone colour is applied at full saturation, then multiplied by zone brightness (0--100%). Black gaps between zones would indicate disabled zones.
Centre Origin Marker White 1px vertical line at x=63 (and continuation at x=64), spanning the full height of the strip preview region. Maps to physical LEDs 79/80 -- the centre of the Light Guide Plate where all effects originate.
Zone Boundary Lines Dim grey (#555555) 1px vertical lines at the LED positions where zones transition. With default 3-zone symmetrical layout: at LED 27/54/105/132, mapping to pixel columns 21/43/84/105.
Zone Info Rows (lower half) Three rows, one per zone. Each contains: colour-coded 4px dot, zone label ("Z1"/"Z2"/"Z3"), and brightness percentage right-aligned. Font: 8px monospace. Spacing: 12px vertical pitch.
Status Indicators (top row) WiFi dot at (4,3), WS dot at (123,3). Both 3px radius. Identical to Design 04 -- consistency across screen designs means the operator never needs to relearn status positions.
Footer (effect + speed) Effect name centred at y=114, speed value at y=124. Both in dim grey (#666666) 8px monospace. Effect name truncated to 18 characters maximum. Speed shown as percentage.
