Design Rationale

Philosophy

Inspired by pro audio rack equipment — information density without clutter. Every pixel earns its place. The dark background (#0a0a0a) is not pure black: the slight warmth prevents the harsh contrast that fatigues eyes in dark environments where LED art is typically used.

Colour System

  • #FFB000 — Amber accent. High visibility on dark backgrounds, reads as "active/important" without the alarm connotation of red. Used for effect name and logo.
  • #FF4444 Zone 1 — warm red, strongest visual weight for the primary zone.
  • #44FF44 Zone 2 — green, maximum contrast against Zone 1.
  • #4488FF Zone 3 / Speed — cool blue, completes the RGB triad. Reused for speed bar to reduce colour count.
  • #333 — Bar backgrounds. Visible enough to show the "empty" portion of bars without competing with filled portions.
  • #222 — 1px borders on bars. Subtle separation without visual noise.

Layout Rationale

  • Row 1 (y=0-15) Header: "ZM" logo provides instant product identity. Connection dots (WiFi + WS) are 4px circles — small enough to not distract, large enough to spot at a glance. Green = connected, red = disconnected.
  • Row 2 (y=16-31) Effect: Amber text maximises readability. Truncation with ellipsis handles long effect names gracefully. This is the most-glanced row during performance.
  • Rows 3-5 (y=32-79) Zones: Each zone gets 16px — enough for a zone number, brightness bar, and numeric value. Zone colours match the physical LED zones for immediate spatial mapping. Bars use proportional width (0-100%) against a dark background track.
  • Row 6 (y=80-95) Master: White bar distinguishes master from zone-specific controls. Full-width bar emphasises its authority over all zones.
  • Row 7 (y=96-111) Speed: Blue bar with "S" label. Speed is secondary to brightness but always visible.
  • Row 8 (y=112-127) Status: "ZONES ON/OFF" is the most important mode indicator — green/red binary state is instantly readable. EdgeMixer mode shown in dim text for reference.

Typography

At 128px wide, font choices are constrained. This mockup uses pixel-aligned rendering at native resolution: 5px wide characters for values, 6px for labels. All text is rendered with integer coordinates to prevent sub-pixel blurring on the AtomS3 LCD.

Accessibility on Hardware

Contrast ratios exceed 7:1 for all active information against #0a0a0a. Zone colours are distinguishable even with common colour vision deficiencies (red/green/blue triad rather than similar hues). Numeric values alongside bars provide redundant encoding — brightness is communicated by both bar length and digits.
