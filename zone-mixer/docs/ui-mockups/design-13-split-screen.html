ZONE MIXER — DESIGN 13

Split Screen Hybrid · 128×128 AtomS3 LCD

Click buttons or press 1-4 keys · Click any 4× canvas to cycle panels

Design 13 Rationale — Split Screen Hybrid

Philosophy

The operator's primary need during a performance is knowing the brightness state of all zones at a glance. Design 13 solves this by permanently dedicating the top 64px to brightness bars — the mixer state never disappears, regardless of what secondary information is showing. The bottom half becomes a carousel of contextual panels, cycled by button press (AtomS3 built-in button or mapped encoder click).

Layout Architecture

  • Top half (y=0–63, permanent): Three horizontal zone brightness bars (stacked, zone-coloured) with numeric values, plus a master bar below. This region is drawn identically across all carousel states. A 1px dark grey divider at y=64 separates the halves.
  • Bottom half (y=65–127, carousel): Cycles between three panels: A (effect name + speed), B (WiFi/WS status + IP), C (EdgeMixer mode + spread/strength).
  • Live overlay: Full 128×128 takeover when an encoder is turned. Shows parameter name at top, 48px-tall value in the centre, a colour bar for visual feedback, and automatically returns to split view after 2 seconds of inactivity.

Colour System

  • #FF4444 Zone 1 — warm red, strongest visual weight for the primary zone.
  • #44FF44 Zone 2 — green, maximum contrast against Zone 1.
  • #4488FF Zone 3 — cool blue, completes the RGB triad.
  • #FFFFFF Master — white distinguishes master from zone-specific bars.
  • #FFB000 Amber accent — effect names, labels, high-visibility elements.
  • #22CC44 Connected / positive state indicators.
  • #CC2222 Disconnected / negative state indicators.
  • #1a1a1a Divider line — subtle separation without visual noise.

Advantage Over Full-Screen Designs

In designs 6 and 8, the brightness bars disappear when viewing network status or effect info. An operator performing a live show must press a button to return to the mixer state — a cognitive interruption. Design 13 eliminates this by keeping the bars permanently visible. Secondary information cycles in the lower half, which the operator can ignore entirely during performance. The live parameter overlay still takes over full-screen for immediate feedback on encoder turns, then auto-returns.

Panel Carousel Indicators

Three dots at y=126 (bottom edge) indicate which carousel panel is active. The active dot is white; inactive dots are dim grey. This is the minimum viable pagination indicator at 128px — anything more complex would waste vertical space that belongs to content.

Accessibility on Hardware

Contrast ratios exceed 7:1 for all active information against #0a0a0a. Zone colours are distinguishable even with common colour vision deficiencies (red/green/blue triad). Numeric values alongside bars provide redundant encoding. The permanent top-half means the most critical information (brightness levels) never requires interaction to access.
