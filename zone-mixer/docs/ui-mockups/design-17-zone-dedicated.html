Design 17 Rationale — Zone-Dedicated Pages

Core Concept

One full page per zone. Instead of cramming 3 zones into tiny horizontal bars, each zone gets the entire 128×128 display. When adjusting Zone 2, switch to page 2 and see everything about that zone at once: brightness, effect, speed, palette, enabled state.

Page Structure

  • Pages 1-3 (Zone pages): Header with zone label + enabled dot. Large 32px brightness value dominates the centre. Brightness bar below. Effect name, speed value, and palette name in the lower section. Background tinted in the zone accent colour at very low opacity.
  • Page 4 (Master): Master brightness at 48px — the largest text in the entire design. Master speed, EdgeMixer mode, and zone count summary. White accent throughout for authority.
  • Page 5 (Status): Connection info, IP address, WebSocket state, zone system enabled/disabled. Diagnostic page for setup and troubleshooting.

Colour System

  • #00E5FF — Zone 1 cyan. Electric, high-energy, reads as "primary zone."
  • #00E676 — Zone 2 green. Distinct from cyan, natural readability.
  • #FF9100 — Zone 3 orange. Warm complement to the cool Z1/Z2 tones.
  • #FFFFFF — Master page. White signals global authority over all zones.
  • #FF6644 — Live overlay accent. Warm coral distinguishes parameter takeover from page content.

Live Overlay

Standard 48px value takeover with 2-second duration. The overlay uses a dark translucent background to dim the current page content, centring the adjusted parameter name and value. Works identically on all 5 pages.

Advantage

Information density per zone is dramatically higher than single-page designs. A user adjusting Zone 2 brightness sees the brightness value at 32px (legible at arm's length), plus effect name, speed, and palette — context that previously required scrolling or mode switching. The trade-off is 5 pages instead of 1, but the scroll encoder makes page switching instantaneous.
